One number most investors never check kills their second deal. (Save this + Repost if useful ♻️) Most people run gross rental yield. That number lies. Here are 7 mistakes real estate investors make before they know it: 1. Gross yield is not net yield. After tax, vacancy, maintenance, and management fees, your 8% listing becomes 4.5% in reality. That's the number that matters. 2. Buying in a suburb they've never visited. A 3% vacancy rate in a report looks very different from 6 empty units in the same building. 3. Overstretching on deal one. One bad tenant or one emergency repair wipes out 6 months of returns. The first deal needs breathing room. 4. No exit strategy before signing. Holding 5 years or 10 changes which property makes sense. Decide that before you buy, not after. 5. Holding costs get ignored every time. Vacancy periods, property tax, insurance, and maintenance. Budget 25% of gross rent before the deal makes sense on paper. 6. Emotion driving the decision. "It's a great area" is not a financial argument. Every deal lives and dies on the numbers. 7. Treating the first property as the retirement plan. One deal is a start. Acting like it's the finish line is the real mistake. The investors who fix these early don't just hold their first property. They buy a second one. Good luck. P.S. The number to check first: net yield after all costs, not the figure on the listing.

Extend Quick way to run the real numbers: take gross annual rent, subtract 25% for all costs, divide by purchase price plus acquisition fees. That's your actual yield. Under 4% and the deal needs a very strong reason to still make sense.
